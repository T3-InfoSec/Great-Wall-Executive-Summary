\vspace{-0.5em}

\noindent\textbf{The Problem}\quad
The rise of ``Not your keys, not your coins'' has created a paradox: self-custody users are increasingly vulnerable to physical coercion---the ``\$5 wrench attack.'' In 2025, wrench attacks surged to record levels, and obscurity-based defenses can backfire catastrophically, incentivizing attackers toward greater violence.

\vspace{0.3em}
\noindent\textbf{Our Solution: Tacit Knowledge-Based Authentication (TKBA)}\\
Great Wall's proprietary TKBA protocol is the \textbf{first and only} system that simultaneously delivers:
\vspace{-0.5em}
\begin{itemize}[nosep,leftmargin=1.5em]
    \item \textbf{Deviceless:} No hardware, seed phrase, or object to steal
    \item \textbf{Individual Custody:} True self-custody---no third-party reliance
    \item \textbf{Anti-Obscure:} Security \textit{increases} as attackers learn the protocol
    \item \textbf{Coercion-Resistant:} Requires time + tacit knowledge, neutralizing wrench attacks
\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.3em}
\noindent\textbf{Business Model}\\
A high-margin subscription marketplace connecting \textbf{Clients} (users needing anonymous, memory-intensive computation) with \textbf{Providers} (PC owners monetizing idle capacity via aggressive referral commission). Providers act as a zero-base-cost sales force, driving viral growth and dramatically lowering CAC.
