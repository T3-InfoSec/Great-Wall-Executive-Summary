\subsection*{The Problem}
The rise of ``Not your keys, not your coins'' has created a paradox: self-custody users are increasingly vulnerable to physical coercion---the ``\$5 wrench attack.'' In 2025, wrench attacks surged to record levels, and obscurity-based defenses (decoy wallets, plausible deniability) can backfire catastrophically, incentivizing attackers toward greater violence.

\subsection*{Our Solution: Tacit Knowledge-Based Authentication (TKBA)}
Great Wall's proprietary TKBA protocol is the \textbf{first and only} system that simultaneously delivers:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Deviceless:} No hardware, seed phrase, or object to steal
    \item \textbf{Individual Custody:} True self-custody---no third-party reliance
    \item \textbf{Anti-Obscure:} Security \textit{increases} as attackers learn the protocol
    \item \textbf{Coercion-Resistant:} Requires time + tacit knowledge, neutralizing wrench attacks
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Business Model}
A high-margin subscription marketplace connecting:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Clients:} Users needing anonymous, memory-intensive computation
    \item \textbf{Providers:} PC owners monetizing idle capacity via aggressive referral commission
\end{itemize}
Providers act as a zero-base-cost sales force, driving viral growth and dramatically lowering CAC.
